### The Crisis: What Happened and Why
The 2008 Great Financial Crisis (GFC) was the most severe global economic downturn since the Great Depression. It was a structural failure of the global banking system caused by years of issuing high-risk mortgages to borrowers with poor credit. These loans were bundled into complex securities and sold worldwide. When house prices stopped rising and borrowers defaulted, these "toxic assets" caused a global liquidity freeze.

> **The "Heart Attack" Moment:** The bankruptcy of Lehman Brothers on September 15, 2008, sent shockwaves through the global financial system, requiring unprecedented government intervention to prevent a total collapse.

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **"The Big Short" Traders:** Hedge fund managers like Michael Burry who bet against the housing market.
- **Discount Retailers:** Companies like Walmart and McDonald's thrived as consumers traded down.
- **The "Big Four" Banks:** Survivors like JPMorgan Chase and Wells Fargo grew larger by acquiring failing rivals.
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Homeowners:** Millions of families lost their homes to foreclosure.
- **Investment Banks:** Lehman Brothers disappeared; Bear Stearns and Merrill Lynch were forced into "shotgun marriages."
- **Retirees:** Trillions in pension and 401(k) value evaporated, delaying retirement for a generation.
</LoserBox>

---

### The Recovery: Timing & Shape
The recovery was a **U-Shaped** "Balance Sheet Recession." Unlike the 1987 crash, consumers and banks spent years paying down debt rather than spending or lending. 

* **The Catalyst:** Massive "Quantitative Easing" (QE) by the Federal Reserve and near-zero interest rates.
* **Recovery Time:** It took the S&P 500 until **March 2013 (roughly 5 years)** to regain its 2007 peak. Unemployment didn't fully recover until 2016.
