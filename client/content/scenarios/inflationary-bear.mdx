### The Crisis: What Happened and Why
The 2021-2022 Bear Market was a fundamental repricing of risk. Following the massive fiscal and monetary stimulus of the COVID-19 era, global supply chains remained snarled while consumer demand surged. This led to "sticky" inflation that forced the Federal Reserve to abandon its "transitory" narrative. As interest rates rose at the fastest pace in decades, the "discount rate" for future earnings increased, causing a massive sell-off in high-growth, unprofitable technology stocks.

> **The Geopolitical Catalyst:** Russia's invasion of Ukraine in February 2022 acted as an accelerant, sending global energy and food prices soaring and cementing the inflationary trend.

---

### Timeline of the Crash
* **November 2021:** The Nasdaq Composite peaks as the Fed hints at "tapering" its bond purchases.
* **January 2022:** The S&P 500 hits its all-time high before starting a jagged descent as inflation readings hit 7.5%.
* **June 2022:** The S&P 500 officially enters a technical Bear Market (down 20% from peaks) following a 75-basis-point rate hike‚Äîthe largest since 1994.
* **October 13, 2022 (The Bottom):** Markets hit their ultimate low following a hot CPI print, before beginning a recovery led by a cooling labor market.

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **Energy Sector:** Oil and Gas giants (Exxon, Chevron) saw record profits as Brent crude spiked above $120.
- **Value Stocks:** "Old Economy" companies with strong cash flows (Consumer Staples, Defense) outperformed significantly.
- **Cash & Short-term T-Bills:** For the first time in a decade, "Cash was not trash" as yields finally moved above 4%.
- **Commodities:** Physical assets like wheat, nickel, and lithium surged during the supply crunch.
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Unprofitable Tech:** "Growth-at-any-cost" companies saw valuations crater by 70-90% (e.g., ARK Innovation holdings).
- **Cryptocurrency:** The "speculative bubble" burst, led by the collapse of Terra/Luna and FTX, wiping out $2 trillion in value.
- **Bond Holders:** 2022 was the worst year in history for the 60/40 portfolio, as both stocks and bonds fell simultaneously.
- **Housing:** Mortgage rates doubled, causing a rapid freeze in the real estate market.
</LoserBox>

---

### The Recovery: The "Checkmark" Shape
The recovery was a grinding climb as the market adjusted to a "Higher for Longer" interest rate environment.



* **The Catalyst:** Inflation began a steady decline from its 9.1% peak, allowing the Fed to eventually pause rate hikes. The explosion of **Generative AI** in early 2023 provided the new growth narrative needed to lead the market to new highs.
* **Recovery Time:** **9 months.** The S&P 500 reached a new all-time high by January 2024, though many individual tech stocks still haven't recovered their 2021 peaks.
