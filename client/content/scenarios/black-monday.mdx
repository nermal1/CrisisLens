### The Crash: What Happened and Why
Black Monday was a sudden, severe, and largely unexpected global stock market crash. It began in Hong Kong, spread through Europe, and culminated in the United States, where the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) plummeted by over 22% in a single trading session. Unlike the 1929 crash, the 1987 event was a sharp, technical "flash" event that did not result in a long-term economic collapse.

> **Why markets fell:** A "perfect storm" of high valuations, geopolitical jitters in the Persian Gulf, and computerized "portfolio insurance" programs that triggered a recursive feedback loop of automatic selling.

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **Short Sellers:** Traders who bet against the market made fortunes in hours.
- **"Dry Powder" Investors:** Cash-heavy investors who bought high-quality stocks at massive discounts.
- **Regulators:** The crash led to the creation of "Circuit Breakers" to prevent future flash crashes.
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Institutional Investors:** Those relying on "portfolio insurance" found no buyers for their automated sell orders.
- **Retail Investors:** Many small investors panicked and sold at the absolute bottom.
- **IPO Candidates:** Over 100 companies cancelled plans to go public in the following months.
</LoserBox>
