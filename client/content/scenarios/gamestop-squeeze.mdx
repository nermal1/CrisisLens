### The Crisis: What Happened and Why
The GameStop squeeze was not driven by macroeconomics, but by a coordinated "David vs. Goliath" narrative. Retail investors on the Reddit community **r/wallstreetbets** identified that GameStop was shorted at over 140% of its float. By buying shares and call options en masse, they forced hedge funds to buy back shares at ballooning prices to cover their losses, sending the stock from under $20 to an intraday high of $483.

> **The Turning Point:** On January 28, 2021, major brokers like Robinhood restricted the purchase of GME, citing clearinghouse collateral requirements. This "buying halt" broke the momentum and caused the price to crater, leading to a massive PR crisis and congressional hearings.

---

### Timeline of the Squeeze
* **January 11, 2021:** GameStop appoints Ryan Cohen to its board, signaling a digital transformation. The stock begins to climb.
* **January 22, 2021:** A "Gamma Squeeze" begins as market makers are forced to buy stock to hedge against massive retail option buying.
* **January 27, 2021 (The Peak):** Elon Musk tweets "Gamestonk!!". Hedge fund Melvin Capital requires a multi-billion dollar bailout.
* **January 28, 2021:** Brokers restrict buying. The stock price crashes as investors can only sell, not buy.
* **February 2021:** The initial bubble bursts, but the "Ape" movement is born, keeping the stock highly volatile for years.

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **Early Retail Adopters:** Investors like Keith Gill ("Roaring Kitty") who turned thousands into tens of millions.
- **GameStop Corp:** The company sold new shares at high prices, raising $1.67 billion and wiping out its debt.
- **High-Frequency Traders:** Firms like Citadel Securities profited from the massive surge in trading volume.
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Short-Sellers:** Hedge funds like Melvin Capital lost 53% of their value in a month and eventually shut down.
- **Late "FOMO" Buyers:** Investors who bought at $300+ and held through the crash, losing 80-90% of their principal.
- **Robinhood's Reputation:** The broker faced intense scrutiny and lost user trust after restricting trades.
</LoserBox>

---

### The Recovery: A "New Normal"
This was not a traditional recovery. The stock price became decoupled from its earnings. While it crashed back to the $40 range (pre-split) within two weeks, it remained a "permanent" meme stock with frequent, unpredictable spikes.



* **The Catalyst:** The momentum was broken by the brokerage "buying halt," allowing institutional shorts to reposition in a less chaotic environment.
* **Recovery Type:** Volatility Spike / Fragmented. The stock never returned to "fundamental" value; it became a vehicle for social sentiment.
