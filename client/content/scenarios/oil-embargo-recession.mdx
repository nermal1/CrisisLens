### The Crisis: What Happened and Why

In 1973, OPEC imposed an oil embargo against the United States and other countries, causing oil prices to quadruple. This led to stagflation — a rare combination of high inflation and economic stagnation.

---

### Timeline of the Crisis

* **Oct 1973:** OPEC announces oil embargo  
* **1974:** Inflation surges, GDP declines  
* **1975:** Recession deepens globally  

---

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **Energy Producers:** Benefited from rising oil prices  
- **Commodity Investors:** Inflation boosted hard assets  
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Consumers:** Faced high fuel costs  
- **Equities:** Declined sharply due to economic slowdown  
</LoserBox>

---

### Lessons for Investors

* 🛢 **Energy shocks ripple through the economy**  
* 📈 **Inflation can coexist with recession**  
* ⚖️ **Diversification across asset classes is key**
