### The Crash: What Happened and Why
On the afternoon of May 6, 2010, the U.S. stock market experienced one of its most terrifying and mysterious events. In just a few minutes, the Dow Jones Industrial Average plummeted nearly 1,000 pointsâ€”at the time the largest intraday point decline in historyâ€”wiping out $1 trillion in market value. 

> **The "Fat Finger" Myth:** Initially blamed on a trader typing a wrong number, the crash was actually caused by a $4.1 billion sell order of E-Mini S&P 500 contracts by a mutual fund (Waddell & Reed). High-frequency trading (HFT) algorithms reacted to this volume by selling aggressively, creating a "hot potato" effect that drained all liquidity from the market.

---

### Timeline of the 36-Minute Chaos
* **2:32 PM:** A large automated sell order for 75,000 E-Mini S&P 500 contracts begins.
* **2:41 PM:** Trading volume spikes. HFT algorithms start selling to each other at lightning speed, driving prices down vertically.
* **2:45 PM:** The "Bottom." Some blue-chip stocks like **Accenture** trade for **$0.01 per share**, while others like **Apple** trade for **$100,000**.
* **2:46 PM:** The Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) triggers a 5-second trading pause. This allows algorithms to "reset."
* **3:00 PM:** The market has recovered roughly 70% of its lightning-fast losses.

### Quick Stats
* **Date:** May 6, 2010.
* **Duration:** 36 minutes from peak to trough to relative recovery.
* **Severity Level:** Medium (Level 6/10). While terrifying, it did not reflect a change in economic fundamentals and was "undone" almost immediately.

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **Nimble Day Traders:** Manual traders who had the "courage" to buy when they saw multi-billion dollar companies trading for pennies.
- **Waddell & Reed:** Despite starting the sell-off, their hedge was executed (albeit at terrible prices).
- **Regulators:** The event forced the SEC to implement "Circuit Breakers" and "Limit Up-Limit Down" rules to prevent a repeat.
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Stop-Loss Holders:** Millions of "safe" sell orders were triggered at the absolute bottom, selling shares for pennies that were worth $50 five minutes later.
- **HFT Firms:** Several high-frequency firms faced massive losses or technical meltdowns during the "liquidity void."
- **ETF Investors:** Many ETFs broke their "peg" to the underlying stocks, trading at 90% discounts temporarily.
</LoserBox>

---

### The Recovery: The 1-Day V-Shape
This was the ultimate **V-Shaped** recovery. Because the crash was purely technical and not based on bad earnings or economic data, prices returned to normal as soon as the algorithms stopped fighting each other.



* **The Catalyst:** A 5-second automatic pause on the CME allowed humans and algorithms to realize that the price drop was a technical glitch, not a world-ending event.
* **Recovery Time:** **1 Day.** By the closing bell, the Dow was only down about 3%, a significant recovery from the 9% drop earlier in the hour.
