### The Crisis: What Happened and Why

In 2011, political conflict in Congress over raising the U.S. debt ceiling brought the government dangerously close to default. The uncertainty shook investor confidence and led to a historic downgrade of U.S. credit by Standard & Poor’s.

---

### Timeline of the Crisis

* **April 2011:** Debt ceiling debates intensify  
* **July 2011:** Treasury warns of imminent default risk  
* **Aug 2, 2011:** Last-minute deal reached  
* **Aug 5, 2011:** S&P downgrades U.S. credit rating  

---

### Winners and Losers

<WinnerBox>
- **Gold Investors:** Safe-haven demand surged  
- **Treasury Bonds:** Paradoxically gained demand despite downgrade  
</WinnerBox>

<LoserBox>
- **Equities:** Broad selloff due to uncertainty  
- **Financial Sector:** Hit hardest from instability fears  
</LoserBox>

### Lessons for Investors

* 🏛 **Political risk matters**

* 📉 **Markets react quickly to policy instability**
